## FRAMEWORKS (thinking tools, not citations)

**Working Backwards (6-Step Commercialization Roadmap)**
1. What does a signed contract with your target customer actually look like?
2. What budget approval process preceded that signature?
3. What IT/security/compliance review came before that?
4. What clinical validation evidence was needed for that review?
5. What regulatory approval was required for that evidence to matter?
6. What MVP and clinical data were needed to get to regulatory approval?

Start at Step 6 (product), and you're building without a destination. Start at Step 1 (the contract), and every decision has commercial logic behind it.

**The Pilot Design Framework**
A pilot without a commercial structure is a science experiment. A well-designed pilot has:
- Outcomes defined in advance that link to a business decision
- Budget awareness from day one
- ALL decision-makers identified and engaged
- A next step already mapped: onboarding, contracting, or scaling planned in parallel

**The Zombie Algorithm Diagnostic**
Before building or pitching an AI product, answer:
- Who signs the contracts?
- How do they make money from your technology?
- What's the business model beyond the pilot?
- Who pays to maintain, update, and retrain the system?

**The Decision-Maker Map**
Clinical stakeholders are advocates. They are rarely the buyers. The real chain:
1. Senior management — authorize projects, hold budget control
2. Finance teams — validate pricing, terms, ROI
3. IT and regulatory — compliance, security, integration
4. Administrative/operational — workflow fit
5. Clinical stakeholders — influencers and champions, but rarely final approvers

**The Four-Question Stakeholder Framework**
- Who is **buying** your innovation?
- Who is **using** your innovation?
- Who is **paying** for your innovation?
- Who **benefits** from your innovation?

**The Four Paths to Commercialization**
1. Insurers and payers (formal reimbursement)
2. Resellers and B2B channel partners
3. Hospitals and healthcare organizations (institutional sales)
4. Device and pharma companies (licensing, integration, co-commercialization)
