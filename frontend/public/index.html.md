## Brilliant products fail without a path to revenue.

Research teams, startups, and established manufacturers develop ground-breaking medical technologies — then falter when it comes time to sell. The gap between a working product and a paying customer is where most health technology ventures lose momentum.

The challenge is rarely the technology itself. It is navigating hospital procurement, meeting regulatory requirements, building reimbursement pathways, and finding the right commercial model for a complex, risk-averse market.

## We work backwards from the sale.

Digital Health Works starts where it matters most: who will buy your product, how they will pay for it, and what it takes to close the deal. We call this approach Value Dynamics — a methodology that maps the revenue pathway before you go to market.

From there, we build the commercial infrastructure around your innovation. Regulatory strategy, distribution channels, hospital relationships, and pricing models — all grounded in the economics of healthcare, not assumptions.

The result is a product that reaches clinicians, improves patient outcomes, and generates sustainable revenue.

## Case Study: AccessMRI — From Concept to Commercial Launch

AccessMRI is a cloud-based SaaS platform for CIED (Cardiac Implantable Electronic Device) MRI safety — providing real-time labeling verification and compatibility assessments for patients with cardiac implants undergoing MRI scans.

Digital Health Works led the full commercialization pathway: product strategy, regulatory positioning (non-SaMD, HIPAA compliant), revenue modeling, distribution strategy, and US market launch — demonstrating our end-to-end capabilities from initial concept through commercial traction.

| Metric | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Markets | US & EU dual-market launch strategy |
| Platform | Cloud-based SaaS on Microsoft Azure |
| Target | Radiology imaging centers & hospitals |
| Compliance | HIPAA-compliant infrastructure |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What types of companies does Digital Health Works work with?

We work with medical device manufacturers, digital health companies, health technology startups, and life science ventures at all stages — from early-stage teams developing prototypes and raising funding, to established manufacturers seeking new market entry in the US or Europe. Our clients span clinical imaging, cardiology, radiology, remote patient monitoring, AI-based diagnostics, and clinical decision support.

### How is Digital Health Works different from a traditional consulting firm?

We do not just advise — we execute. As a Manufacturer's Sales Representative, we put skin in the game alongside our clients. Our Value Dynamics methodology grounds every recommendation in real-world healthcare economics, and our Market Development team carries that strategy through regulatory, distribution, and hospital procurement. Strategy and execution are integrated, not handed off.

### What regulatory frameworks does Digital Health Works cover?

We navigate both US and European regulatory pathways. On the US side, this includes FDA 510(k), De Novo classification, and SaMD (Software as a Medical Device) positioning. In Europe, we cover EU MDR compliance, CE marking, and notified body engagement. We also address adjacent requirements including HIPAA, CPT billing codes, and health technology assessment (HTA) submissions.

### Where is Digital Health Works based?

We are headquartered in Berlin, Germany, with direct operational reach across both European and US markets. Our team works across time zones and has on-the-ground experience with hospital systems, regulatory bodies, and distribution networks on both continents.

### How do I get started with Digital Health Works?

Schedule a call through our website. We begin every engagement with a discovery conversation to understand your product, your market ambitions, and where you are in the commercialization journey. From there, we recommend a tailored engagement — whether that is strategic consulting, market development support, or full sales representation.
