## Our Approach

We start from the sale and work backward.

Most medical technologies fail at commercialization, not at the bench. The gap between a working product and a paying customer is where ventures lose momentum — navigating hospital procurement, building reimbursement pathways, and finding the right commercial model for a risk-averse market.

Digital Health Works starts where it matters most: who will buy your product, how they will pay for it, and what it takes to close the deal. We call this Value Dynamics — a methodology that maps the revenue pathway before you go to market, then builds the commercial infrastructure to execute on it.

## Case Study: AccessMRI — From Concept to Commercial Launch

AccessMRI is a cloud-based SaaS platform for CIED (Cardiac Implantable Electronic Device) MRI safety — providing real-time labeling verification and compatibility assessments for patients with cardiac implants undergoing MRI scans.

Digital Health Works led the full commercialization pathway: product strategy, market positioning, revenue modeling, distribution strategy, and US market launch — demonstrating our end-to-end capabilities from initial concept through commercial traction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What types of companies does Digital Health Works work with?

We work with medical device manufacturers, digital health companies, health technology startups, and life science ventures at all stages — from early-stage teams developing prototypes and raising funding, to established manufacturers seeking new market entry in the US or Europe.

### How is Digital Health Works different from a traditional consulting firm?

We do not just advise — we execute. As a Manufacturer's Sales Representative, we put skin in the game alongside our clients. Our Value Dynamics methodology grounds every recommendation in real-world healthcare economics, and our Market Development team carries that strategy through distribution, hospital procurement, and commercial execution.

### What is Value Dynamics?

Value Dynamics is our health economics and value strategy framework. It works backwards from the point of sale — analyzing the economic, clinical, and institutional forces that determine adoption. This includes mapping reimbursement pathways, modeling budget impact, identifying purchasing stakeholders, and building the value framework that procurement committees and payers need.

### Where is Digital Health Works based?

We are headquartered in Berlin, Germany, with direct operational reach across both European and US markets. Our team works across time zones and has on-the-ground experience with hospital systems, distribution networks, and commercial operations on both continents.
